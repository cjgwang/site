I have an invisible disability that greatly affects my life, and I wish more people would talk about disabilities in general! I hope to shed a bit of light onto my condition and list some accommodations that would help me.

I have the commonly meme'd scoliosis (which I don't find very funny) and used to have spinal arthritis. 

I spent the ages of 12-17 wearing a scoliosis brace, every day for 21 hours/day. A scoliosis brace is moulded to your body, made of hard plastic, and designed to correct any curvature. As as insecure teenager, it was probably the worst time to be wearing a full body brace.
![[Pasted image 20250818110040.png]]
I used to wear something chonkier like [this one](https://scoliosisbracecanada.com/our-braces/) from Scoliosis Brace Canada. 

Then, as medical technology got better (yay), I got a lighter one like [this](https://www.indiamart.com/proddetail/scoliosis-wcr-brace-22411840230.html).
![[Pasted image 20250818110319.png]]
(Imagine going to school in the summer and sweating in this...)

My disability is invisible, which is a great privilege that comes with downsides. It means that I still have able-bodied privilege. On the other hand, it means that people often tell me I am not disabled, or dismiss my accessibility needs.

Here are things people can do to help me out:
- Offer me seats on public transportation/meeting rooms
- Schedule events closer to me, so that I don't have to travel for long distances, and earlier in the day, since that is when I have the most energy
- Take more breaks during meetings, trips, and hangouts and understand if I have to leave early
- Provide more ergonomic/comfier chairs or beds
- Ensure that meeting spaces are accessible without too many stairs

Thank you!
