TLDR: No

Cath wants to say: *This will benefit you most if you are an EA/AI safety person and thinking about what to apply for and switch courses. Reading my post on [[PPE Courses]] might be helpful.*

I applied for PPE because I really cared about topics like global health and development, animal welfare, poverty, and war. I was extremely depressed and found philosophy to be my only reprise, and I asked myself, what does it mean to live a moral life? I also knew that PPE at Oxford was the "EA degree" and knew a lot of cool smart EAs who did PPE. 

I initially wanted to switch courses to maths at the start of the year. I decided against that.

Reasons I wanted to study PPE at Oxford (compared to a technical subject)
- I am good at my subject. I received a collections prize and did pretty well on my prelims. I think I could get a first in PPE, but I don't think I could have competed with the IMO/math olympiad kids and gotten a First for my prelims or finals.
- I am able to complete my PPE work quickly. For my first year, that was 2 essays a week, 1 problem sheet, and three 1-2h tutorials. This gives me a lot of free time to work on stuff that I care about. 
- I don't think that studying PPE has significantly stopped me from getting an AI safety job. I've done pretty interesting research, been invited to work on AI safety in the Bay Area, been accepted to technical camps, etc. 
	- I think that I would probably get more opportunities if I studied maths? But I am not sure. 
- I am quite risk averse. 
	- Since I was set on PPE while applying, I didn't have much maths supra-curricular activities that would have made me stand out, so I probably wouldn't have gotten accepted. On the other hand, I was fairly sure that my PPE application would be very competitive. 
	- I did not want to switch because I didn't know whether that would be possible. My tutors strongly advised me against switching and told me it was very impractical. I also didn't know if I would like studying maths full time, or whether I would get a first. 

Reasons I did not want to study PPE at Oxford (compared to a technical subject)
- I didn't like economics as much as I thought I would, and I ended up picking the technical papers anyway.
- I am pretty good at technical stuff, so 
- Signalling purposes. I think that a maths/CS degree from Oxford has a much stronger signalling effect for technical jobs (mathsphil and CSphil maybe equal or slightly less).
