 TLDR: No

Cath wants to say: *This will benefit you most if you are an EA/AI safety person and thinking about what to apply for and switch courses. Reading my post on [[PPE Courses]] might be helpful.*

I applied for PPE because I really cared about topics like global health and development, animal welfare, poverty, and war. I was extremely depressed and found philosophy to be my only reprise, and I asked myself, what does it mean to live a moral life? I also knew that PPE at Oxford was the "EA degree" and I was a very committed EA. 

I initially wanted to switch courses to maths at the start of the year. I decided against that and did not switch.

Reasons I wanted to study PPE at Oxford (compared to a technical subject)
- I am good at my subject. I received a collections prize and did pretty well on my prelims. I think I could get a first in PPE, but I don't think I could have competed with the IMO/math olympiad kids and gotten a First for my prelims or finals.
- I am able to complete my PPE work quickly. For my first year, that was 2 essays a week, 1 problem sheet, and three 1-2h tutorials. This gives me a lot of free time to work on stuff that I care about.
