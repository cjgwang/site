Here are some of my favourite poems that I like to revisit once in a while. 

[Litany in Which Certain Things Are Crossed Out, Richard Siken](https://www.poetryfoundation.org/poems/48158/litany-in-which-certain-things-are-crossed-out)

> If the window is on your right, you are in your own bed. If the window 
>            is over your heart, and it is painted shut, then we are breathing  
>river water.  
>Build me a city and call it Jerusalem. Build me another and call it                                                                                            
> Jerusalem.

(Also check out Siken's *War of the Foxes* and *Crush*, of course.)

[in love as in tourism, Xiao Yue Shan](https://www.poetryfoundation.org/poetrymagazine/poems/155667/in-love-as-in-tourism)

>cartography seems the strangest science today  
>as morning alters the fittings of the hour to form  
>shapes wholly new. 

[A Memory of Us, Safia Elhillo](https://www.poetryfoundation.org/poems/159491/a-memory-of-us)

> i downloaded an app 
> & learned their names: beautyberry, yarrow,
> cornus florida, black-eyed susan, & you,
> & you, my bright hibiscus, my every color

[Waiting by Noor Naga](https://www.poetryfoundation.org/poems/155342/waiting-2)

> There are people to live for and people to die            for 
> I comfort myself: there are people to sleep with and people to wake with
